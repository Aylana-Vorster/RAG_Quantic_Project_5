
# Sandbox Travel Policy

## 1. Introduction

This policy outlines procedures for employees who travel for business purposes. The goal is to ensure that travel is safe, productive, and cost-effective. This policy should be read in conjunction with the Sandbox Expense Policy.

## 2. Booking Travel

- **Approval:** All business travel must be approved in advance by your manager.
- **Preferred Vendors:** Employees should use the company's designated travel agency and preferred vendors for booking flights and accommodation wherever possible.
- **Booking in Advance:** To secure better rates, travel should be booked at least 14 days in advance.

## 3. Flights

- **Class of Travel:** All employees are expected to book economy class tickets for both domestic and international flights.
- **Baggage:** The company will cover the cost of one checked bag. Any additional baggage fees for personal items are the employee's responsibility.

## 4. Accommodation

- **Hotel Class:** Employees should book business-class hotels that are safe and reasonably priced.
- **Limits:** Hotel costs should not exceed the limits specified in the expense policy for the city of travel.
- **Personal Stays:** If an employee wishes to extend a business trip for personal reasons, they will be responsible for the cost of their accommodation during the personal portion of the stay.

## 5. Ground Transportation

- **Rental Cars:** A rental car may be approved if it is the most cost-effective means of transportation. Employees should rent mid-size or smaller vehicles.
- **Taxis and Ride-Sharing:** Taxis or ride-sharing services (like Uber or Bolt) should be used for transport to and from airports, and for other travel where public transport is not practical.

## 6. Travel Insurance

The company provides comprehensive travel insurance for all employees on international business trips. Details of the policy can be obtained from HR.

## 7. Safety and Security

- **Itinerary:** Employees should ensure their manager has a copy of their travel itinerary.
- **Emergency Contacts:** Keep a list of emergency contacts, including the company's travel assistance provider.
- **Health:** Consult a doctor for any required vaccinations or health precautions before traveling internationally.

## 8. Non-Reimbursable Travel Expenses

- In-room movies
- Mini-bar purchases (unless for a business meeting)
- Laundry services (for trips shorter than 5 days)
- Gym or spa fees
