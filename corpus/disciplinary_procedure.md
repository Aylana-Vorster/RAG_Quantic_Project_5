
# Sandbox Disciplinary Procedure (South Africa)

## 1. Introduction

Sandbox believes in a fair and consistent approach to discipline. The purpose of this procedure is to ensure that all employees are treated fairly and that disciplinary action is taken only when necessary. This procedure is guided by the principles of the Labour Relations Act and the Code of Good Practice on dismissals.

## 2. Principles of a Fair Procedure

- The employee should be aware of the rule they have allegedly broken.
- The investigation must be conducted impartially.
- The employee has the right to state their case and be represented by a fellow employee or trade union representative.
- The disciplinary penalty must be appropriate for the offence.

## 3. Informal vs. Formal Action

- **Informal Action (Counselling):** For minor misconduct, the first step is usually informal counselling by the manager.
- **Formal Action:** For more serious offences or repeated misconduct, a formal disciplinary process will be followed.

## 4. Formal Disciplinary Procedure

**Step 1: Notification of a Hearing**
- The employee will receive a written notice at least 48 hours before the hearing.
- The notice will specify the charges, the date, time, and place of the hearing, and the employee's right to representation.

**Step 2: The Disciplinary Hearing**
- The hearing will be chaired by an impartial manager.
- The company will present its case and evidence.
- The employee will have the opportunity to respond, present evidence, and cross-examine witnesses.

**Step 3: The Verdict and Sanction**
- After the hearing, the chairperson will decide whether the employee is guilty of the alleged misconduct.
- If found guilty, a suitable sanction will be imposed. Sanctions may include:
    - **Verbal Warning:** For minor infractions.
    - **Written Warning:** Valid for 6 months.
    - **Final Written Warning:** Valid for 12 months.
    - **Dismissal:** For the most serious offences (e.g., theft, fraud, gross insubordination) or after a series of warnings have failed to correct behavior.

**Step 4: Appeal**
- The employee has the right to appeal the decision within 5 working days.
- The appeal will be heard by a more senior manager.

## 5. Dismissal

- **Misconduct:** Dismissal for misconduct will only occur after a fair disciplinary process.
- **Incapacity (Poor Performance):** Before dismissal for poor performance, the employee must be given appropriate evaluation, instruction, training, guidance, or counselling.
- **Incapacity (Ill Health):** We will try to accommodate an employee who is unable to perform their duties due to ill health, but if this is not possible, a dismissal for incapacity may be considered.
- **Retrenchment (Operational Requirements):** Dismissals due to operational requirements will follow the procedures outlined in the Labour Relations Act.

## 6. Employee Rights

Throughout this process, employees have the right to a fair hearing, representation, and to appeal any decision.
