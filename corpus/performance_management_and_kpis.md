
# Sandbox Performance Management and KPIs

## 1. Introduction

Sandbox is a results-oriented company. We believe in setting clear goals, providing regular feedback, and recognizing and rewarding performance. This document outlines our approach to performance management and the use of Key Performance Indicators (KPIs).

## 2. Performance Cycle

Our performance cycle runs annually from January 1st to December 31st. The cycle consists of four key stages:

1.  **Goal Setting (January):** Employees and managers work together to define individual and team KPIs for the year.
2.  **Mid-Year Review (July):** A formal check-in to discuss progress, provide feedback, and make any necessary adjustments to KPIs.
3.  **End-of-Year Review (December):** A comprehensive review of performance against KPIs, including a self-assessment and a manager's assessment.
4.  **Ongoing Feedback:** We encourage a culture of continuous feedback. Managers should provide regular, informal feedback, and employees should feel comfortable seeking it.

## 3. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- **Definition:** KPIs are quantifiable measures used to evaluate the success of an individual, team, or the company in meeting its objectives.
- **SMART Goals:** KPIs should be SMART:
    - **S**pecific: Clear and well-defined.
    - **M**easurable: Can be tracked and quantified.
    - **A**chievable: Realistic and attainable.
    - **R**elevant: Aligned with the company's strategic goals.
    - **T**ime-bound: Has a defined timeline.
- **Examples of KPIs:**
    - **Sales:** Revenue generated, number of new clients, conversion rate.
    - **Engineering:** Sprint velocity, bug resolution time, system uptime.
    - **Support:** Customer satisfaction (CSAT) score, first response time.

## 4. Performance Ratings

At the end-of-year review, employees will receive a performance rating. The rating scale is as follows:

- **5 - Exceeds Expectations:** Consistently performs far beyond the requirements of the role.
- **4 - Meets All, Exceeds Some:** Meets all KPI targets and exceeds some.
- **3 - Meets Expectations:** Consistently meets KPI targets.
- **2 - Needs Improvement:** Fails to meet some KPI targets.
- **1 - Unacceptable Performance:** Fails to meet the majority of KPI targets.

## 5. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

- An employee who receives a rating of "Needs Improvement" or "Unacceptable Performance" may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).
- A PIP is a formal document that outlines specific areas for improvement and provides a clear timeline and support for the employee to get back on track.

## 6. Tying Performance to Rewards

Performance ratings will be a key factor in determining salary increases, bonuses, and opportunities for promotion.
