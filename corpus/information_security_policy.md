
# Sandbox Information Security Policy

## 1. Introduction

The purpose of this Information Security Policy is to protect Sandbox's information assets from security threats, whether internal or external, deliberate or accidental. This policy applies to all Sandbox employees, contractors, and other authorized users of our information systems.

## 2. Roles and Responsibilities

- **All Employees:** All employees are responsible for understanding and adhering to this policy, and for reporting any security incidents.
- **IT Department:** The IT department is responsible for implementing and maintaining security controls, monitoring for threats, and responding to incidents.
- **Management:** Management is responsible for ensuring that all employees are aware of and comply with this policy.

## 3. Acceptable Use

- **Company Assets:** Company-provided equipment, including laptops, phones, and software, are to be used for business purposes. Limited personal use may be acceptable, but it should not interfere with your job responsibilities or violate any other company policies.
- **Passwords:** You are responsible for keeping your passwords confidential. Passwords must meet the company's complexity requirements and should be changed regularly.
- **Email and Internet:** Use of company email and internet should be for business purposes. Do not use company systems to access or transmit inappropriate or illegal material.

## 4. Data Classification and Handling

- **Confidential:** Highly sensitive data that could cause significant harm to Sandbox or its clients if disclosed. Examples include financial data, client data, and employee PII. Access is restricted to authorized personnel only.
- **Internal:** Data that is for internal use only and not intended for public disclosure. Examples include internal reports and project plans.
- **Public:** Data that is freely available to the public.

All employees are responsible for handling data in accordance with its classification.

## 5. Security Measures

- **Access Control:** Access to information systems is granted on a need-to-know basis.
- **Encryption:** Sensitive data must be encrypted both in transit and at rest.
- **Anti-Malware:** All company-provided computers must have approved anti-malware software installed and enabled.
- **VPN:** A Virtual Private Network (VPN) must be used when accessing the company network from an untrusted network (e.g., public Wi-Fi).

## 6. Incident Response

If you suspect a security incident has occurred (e.g., a lost or stolen laptop, a phishing attempt, a data breach), you must report it immediately to the IT department.

## 7. Policy Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
