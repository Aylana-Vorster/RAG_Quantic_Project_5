
# Sandbox Social Media Policy

## 1. Introduction

This policy provides guidance for employees on the use of social media, both personally and professionally. At Sandbox, we trust our employees to be responsible and professional in their online conduct.

## 2. Official Company Social Media

- **Authorized Spokespeople:** Only authorized individuals may speak on behalf of Sandbox on our official social media channels (e.g., LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook).
- **Brand Guidelines:** All posts on official channels must adhere to Sandbox's brand and marketing guidelines.
- **Crisis Communication:** In the event of a public relations crisis, do not post about the issue. All communication will be handled by the designated crisis management team.

## 3. Personal Social Media Use

- **Identify Yourself:** If you identify yourself as a Sandbox employee on your personal social media profiles, you should make it clear that your views are your own and not those of the company. A simple disclaimer like "Opinions are my own" is recommended.
- **Confidentiality:** Do not post or share confidential company information, including information about our clients, financials, or future business plans.
- **Respect:** Be respectful to the company, our clients, your colleagues, and our competitors. Do not engage in arguments or post content that could be seen as harassment, bullying, or discrimination.
- **Reputation:** Remember that your online activity can reflect on Sandbox. Conduct yourself professionally and avoid posting content that could damage the company's reputation.
- **No Endorsements:** Do not use the company name or logo to endorse any product, service, or political position.

## 4. Best Practices for Social Media

- **Think before you post:** Once something is online, it can be difficult to remove.
- **Privacy settings:** Use privacy settings to control who can see your content.
- **Be accurate:** If you post about our industry, make sure your information is accurate.
- **Correct mistakes:** If you make a mistake, admit it and correct it quickly.

## 5. Reporting Concerns

If you see any social media activity that concerns you or violates this policy, please report it to your manager or HR.

## 6. Policy Violations

Violations of this policy may lead to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
